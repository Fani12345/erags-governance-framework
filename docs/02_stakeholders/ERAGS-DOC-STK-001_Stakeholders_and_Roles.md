# Stakeholders and Roles for ERAGS

## 1. Purpose

This document identifies the key stakeholders for the Electrical Roads Asset Governance & Strategy System (ERAGS) and defines their roles and responsibilities at a governance level. It forms the basis for assigning responsibilities in governance processes and requirements.

## 2. Stakeholder List

| ID     | Stakeholder Group                          | Role / Interest                                                     |
|--------|--------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------|
| STK-01 | Roads Directorate Executive Management     | Overall accountability, strategic direction, budget approval        |
| STK-02 | Director – Roads Network Maintenance       | Operational responsibility for network maintenance and performance  |
| STK-03 | Electrical Engineer – Roads Electrical Sub-Section | System owner for ERAGS; technical authority for electrical assets |
| STK-04 | Roads Electrical Operations Staff (Technicians, SAPs) | Implement governance decisions in day-to-day operations        |
| STK-05 | Road Fund / Funding Authority              | Funding provision, value-for-money oversight                        |
| STK-06 | Utility / Electricity Supplier             | Provision of electrical supply, metering and tariffs                |
| STK-07 | Traffic Police / Enforcement Authorities   | Enforcement, road safety oversight, incident response               |
| STK-08 | Municipalities / Local Authorities         | Interface with local street networks and public-realm requirements  |
| STK-09 | Road Users and General Public              | Safety, security, service quality and complaint feedback            |
| STK-10 | Contractors and Consultants                | Design and delivery of projects and maintenance services            |

## 3. Roles in ERAGS Governance

### 3.1 Roads Directorate Executive Management (STK-01)

- Approves the overall asset management policy for road lighting and signals.
- Endorses the Strategic Asset Management Plan (SAMP) for electrical assets.
- Provides strategic direction on risk appetite and service-level expectations.

### 3.2 Director – Roads Network Maintenance (STK-02)

- Acts as the senior accountable owner for electrical road assets.
- Approves ERAGS governance procedures and major lifecycle decisions.
- Chairs or delegates an Asset Management Committee for electrical assets.

### 3.3 Electrical Engineer – Roads Electrical Sub-Section (STK-03)

- Owns and maintains ERAGS documentation and governance processes.
- Ensures that asset-related decisions are informed by reliable data and analysis.
- Coordinates with operations, safety and finance functions to implement policies and strategies.

### 3.4 Roads Electrical Operations Staff (STK-04)

- Execute maintenance and renewal works in accordance with approved policies and strategies.
- Provide feedback on asset condition, failure modes and operational constraints.
- Participate in incident reporting and continuous improvement.

### 3.5 Road Fund / Funding Authority (STK-05)

- Reviews and approves funding proposals derived from the SAMP.
- Monitors performance against agreed indicators for funded programmes.
- Requires traceable justification for major investment decisions.

### 3.6 Utility / Electricity Supplier (STK-06)

- Provides and maintains electrical supply to designated points.
- Collaborates on connection agreements, metering configurations and tariff structures.
- Provides data required for verification of energy consumption and billing.

### 3.7 Traffic Police / Enforcement Authorities (STK-07)

- Provide input on road-safety priorities and hazardous locations.
- Coordinate during major outages or incidents affecting public safety.
- Support enforcement related to vandalism and theft of infrastructure.

### 3.8 Municipalities / Local Authorities (STK-08)

- Provide local planning input (land use changes, developments).
- Coordinate on boundary responsibilities between national and municipal roads.
- Provide feedback on public lighting and traffic-signal performance.

### 3.9 Road Users and General Public (STK-09)

- Report faults, safety concerns and perceived service gaps.
- Provide feedback that can be used in prioritisation and level-of-service decisions.

### 3.10 Contractors and Consultants (STK-10)

- Deliver projects and services that comply with ERAGS policies and standards.
- Provide as-built data and documentation required for asset records.
- Participate in lessons-learned and performance reviews.
